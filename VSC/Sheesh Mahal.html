SHEESH MAHAL

About Sheesh Mahal

The Sheesh Mahal, also known as the Palace of Mirrors, is a magnificent example of Mughal architecture, located within the Lahore Fort in Pakistan. Built in the 1630s during the reign of Emperor Shah Jahan, the palace is renowned for its intricate mirror work (known as "ayina kari") that adorns its walls and ceilings. The small mirrors, embedded in mosaic patterns, create a dazzling effect when illuminated by light, giving the palace its name. The structure was originally intended as a private residence for the royal family and features a blend of Persian, Indian, and Islamic architectural influences. The Sheesh Mahal's beauty lies in its delicate ornamentation, including frescoes, floral motifs, and carved marble panels, making it a prime example of the grandeur of Mughal art. The reflection of candlelight off the mirrors would have created an ethereal, glittering atmosphere during royal gatherings. Today, it remains a UNESCO World Heritage site and one of the most visited landmarks in Lahore, drawing tourists who are captivated by its artistic and historical significance.
