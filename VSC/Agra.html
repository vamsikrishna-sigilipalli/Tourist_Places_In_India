About Agra

Agra, located in the northern Indian state of Uttar Pradesh, is renowned for its rich history and architectural marvels, most notably the Taj Mahal. This UNESCO World Heritage Site, built in the 17th century by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan as a mausoleum for his beloved wife Mumtaz Mahal, is a stunning example of Mughal architecture, combining Persian, Islamic, and Indian styles. The Taj Mahal attracts millions of visitors each year, celebrated for its magnificent white marble facade, intricate inlay work, and stunning gardens, symbolizing love and beauty. Besides the Taj Mahal, Agra is home to other historical sites, such as the Agra Fort and the tomb of Itimad-ud-Daulah, further showcasing its historical significance. In addition to its architectural wonders, Agra plays a vital role in India's tourism and economy. The city is a hub for local handicrafts, particularly marble inlay work, leather goods, and textiles, contributing to the livelihoods of many artisans. Agra's strategic location near major cities like Delhi and Jaipur makes it an integral part of the Golden Triangle tourist circuit, which attracts both domestic and international tourists. The city's vibrant culture, delicious Mughlai cuisine, and bustling bazaars provide visitors with an immersive experience, making Agra not just a destination for history lovers, but also a vibrant city reflecting India's diverse heritage.
