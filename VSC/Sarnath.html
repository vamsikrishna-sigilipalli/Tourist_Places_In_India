About Sarnath

Sarnath, located near Varanasi in Uttar Pradesh, India, is one of the most significant Buddhist pilgrimage sites in the world. It is renowned as the place where Siddhartha Gautama, the Buddha, delivered his first sermon after attaining enlightenment around the 5th century BCE. This sermon, known as the "Dhammacakkappavattana Sutta" or the "Setting in Motion the Wheel of Dharma," introduced the Four Noble Truths and the Eightfold Path, which form the foundation of Buddhist teachings. The Dhamekh Stupa, a prominent structure in Sarnath, marks the exact location of this momentous event and is a focal point for visitors and pilgrims alike. In addition to its historical and religious significance, Sarnath is home to several archaeological treasures, including ancient monasteries, stupas, and ruins that date back to the Mauryan period. The site is part of the Archaeological Survey of India and has been recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage Site.
