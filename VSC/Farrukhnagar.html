About Farrukhnagar

Farrukhnagar is a historic town located in the Gurugram district of Haryana, India. Established in the 18th century by Nawab Farrukh Shah, it served as an important trade center and a prominent urban settlement during the Mughal era. The town is characterized by its rich architectural heritage, including several notable structures such as the tomb of Nawab Farrukh Shah and the ancient mosque, which showcase the Indo-Islamic architectural style. The layout of the town, with its well-planned streets and residential areas, reflects the urban planning of that period, making it a point of interest for historians and tourists alike. Today, Farrukhnagar retains its historical charm while being in proximity to the rapidly developing urban landscape of Gurugram. The town is not only a reminder of its glorious past but also serves as a cultural hub for local traditions and customs. The local economy primarily relies on agriculture and small-scale industries, but it has also started seeing an influx of modern developments. Despite this, Farrukhnagar has managed to preserve its heritage, making it a fascinating destination for those interested in history, architecture, and culture.
