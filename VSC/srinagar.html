About Srinagar

Srinagar, the summer capital of Jammu and Kashmir, is a city known for its stunning natural beauty, rich history, and vibrant culture. Located in the Kashmir Valley on the banks of the Jhelum River, it is famous for its serene Dal Lake and Nigeen Lake, where houseboats and shikaras (traditional wooden boats) offer a unique experience to tourists. The city's Mughal gardens, such as Shalimar Bagh, Nishat Bagh, and Chashme Shahi, reflect the grandeur of the Mughal era, and its historical sites, including the Shankaracharya Temple and Hazratbal Shrine, add spiritual significance. Srinagar's charm is further enhanced by its crafts and cuisine. The city is renowned for its Kashmiri shawls, carpets, and intricate handicrafts, along with the famous saffron and dried fruits. The local cuisine, especially dishes like Rogan Josh, Yakhni, and Kashmiri Kahwa, offers a rich culinary experience. During winter, the nearby Gulmarg ski resort attracts visitors, making Srinagar a year-round destination for nature lovers and adventure seekers alike.
