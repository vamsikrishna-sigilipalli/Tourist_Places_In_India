About Golden Temple (Harmandir Sahib), Amritsar

The Golden Temple, also known as Harmandir Sahib, is the holiest shrine of Sikhism, located in Amritsar, Punjab. Constructed in 1604 by Guru Arjan, the fifth Sikh Guru, the temple symbolizes humility and equality. It is a beautiful structure surrounded by a sacred water tank (Amrit Sarovar), where pilgrims take a dip as part of their spiritual cleansing. The templeâ€™s dome is gilded with gold, giving it its iconic appearance, and its architecture incorporates both Hindu and Islamic styles, reflecting the inclusive nature of Sikhism. Besides its spiritual significance, the Golden Temple is a symbol of community and service. The temple complex houses the world's largest free kitchen (Langar), serving meals to thousands of visitors daily, irrespective of their religion, caste, or background. The atmosphere within the temple complex is peaceful and meditative, offering a space for reflection and devotion. As a major pilgrimage site, it attracts millions of visitors worldwide, making it a focal point of faith, history, and cultural heritage.
