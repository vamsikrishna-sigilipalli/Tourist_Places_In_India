ANANDPUR SAHIB

About Anandpur Sahib

Anandpur Sahib, located in the state of Punjab, India, is a sacred city deeply associated with Sikh history and spirituality. It was founded in 1665 by the ninth Sikh Guru, Guru Tegh Bahadur. Anandpur Sahib is renowned as the birthplace of the Khalsa, the warrior-saint community, which was established by Guru Gobind Singh in 1699. The city is a significant pilgrimage site for Sikhs, with many gurdwaras (Sikh temples) scattered across the region, most notably the Takht Sri Keshgarh Sahib, one of the five sacred seats of authority in Sikhism. Anandpur Sahib is also a symbol of Sikh resistance and the spirit of freedom, commemorating the struggles of the Gurus against Mughal oppression. The city hosts the annual Hola Mohalla festival, a vibrant event where Sikhs display martial arts, take part in religious processions, and celebrate their heritage. Anandpur Sahib is not only important religiously but also historically, as it was the site of key events during the resistance to Mughal rule. The surrounding areas are lush with natural beauty, adding a serene backdrop to its spiritual significance. Visitors come to reflect on Sikh values of justice, equality, and bravery, making Anandpur Sahib a place where faith, history, and nature converge.
