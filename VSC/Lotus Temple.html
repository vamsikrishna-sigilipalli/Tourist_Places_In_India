LOTUS TEMPLE

About Lotus Temple

The Lotus Temple, located in Delhi, India, is a prominent Bahá'í House of Worship known for its unique flower-like design, symbolizing purity and peace. Completed in 1986, the temple is made of white marble and features 27 free-standing petals arranged in a circular pattern. Surrounded by lush gardens and nine pools, it serves as a tranquil space for meditation and reflection, attracting millions of visitors each year. Unlike traditional places of worship, the Lotus Temple emphasizes the oneness of humanity and encourages visitors of all faiths to engage in prayer and contemplation. Architecturally, the Lotus Temple is a marvel of modern design, blending natural elements with contemporary aesthetics. Its structure is not only visually stunning but also utilizes natural light to illuminate the interior, creating a serene atmosphere. The temple’s design is inspired by the lotus flower, a symbol of beauty and enlightenment in various cultures. As a center for spiritual gatherings and cultural events, the Lotus Temple promotes the Bahá'í principles of unity, equality, and the pursuit of peace, making it a significant landmark in both Delhi and the broader context of interfaith dialogue.
