HARIKE WETLAND AND BIRD SANCTUARY

About Harike Wetland and Bird Sanctuary

Harike Wetland and Bird Sanctuary, located at the confluence of the Sutlej and Beas rivers in Punjab, India, is a vital ecological hotspot recognized for its rich biodiversity. Spanning approximately 41 square kilometers, it is the largest wetland in northern India and serves as a crucial habitat for a variety of avian species. The sanctuary is home to over 200 species of birds, including migratory birds that flock here during the winter months. This unique ecosystem not only supports a diverse range of flora and fauna but also plays a significant role in water conservation and flood control in the region. The wetland is also of great importance for conservation efforts, providing critical breeding and feeding grounds for many endangered and vulnerable bird species. It serves as a major stopover point for migratory birds on their journey between breeding and wintering grounds. Moreover, Harike Wetland is a significant site for ecotourism, attracting birdwatchers and nature enthusiasts who come to witness its stunning natural beauty and diverse wildlife. Conservation initiatives are vital to preserving this delicate ecosystem, ensuring its sustainability for future generations while promoting environmental awareness and education.
