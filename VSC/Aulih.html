About Auli

Auli, often referred to as the "snow paradise" of India, is a popular hill station located in the northern state of Uttarakhand. Nestled at an altitude of approximately 2,500 meters, it is renowned for its stunning views of the Nanda Devi and other majestic peaks of the Himalayas. Auli is particularly famous among adventure enthusiasts for its skiing opportunities, with well-maintained slopes that attract both beginners and experienced skiers. The best time to visit Auli for skiing is from late December to February when the area is blanketed in snow. In addition to skiing, visitors can enjoy activities like snowboarding, trekking, and mountain biking during the warmer months. The region is also rich in natural beauty, featuring lush meadows, coniferous forests, and vibrant flora and fauna. Auli serves as a gateway to several treks in the surrounding areas, making it a perfect destination for nature lovers and trekkers. The Auli Artificial Lake, one of the highest man-made lakes in the world, is another major attraction, providing a stunning backdrop for photography and relaxation. With its blend of adventure sports and serene landscapes, Auli offers a unique experience for tourists seeking both thrill and tranquility.
