RANJIT SAGAR DAM

About Ranjit Sagar Dam

The Ranjit Sagar Dam, also known as the Ranjit Sagar Multipurpose Dam, is a significant hydroelectric project located on the Ravi River in the Pathankot district of Punjab, India. Completed in 2001, it stands as one of the largest earthfill dams in India, with a height of 160 meters and a reservoir capacity of about 3,200 million cubic meters. The dam serves multiple purposes, including irrigation, hydroelectric power generation, and flood control. It plays a crucial role in supporting agriculture in the surrounding regions by providing a reliable water supply. In addition to its practical uses, the Ranjit Sagar Dam is a hub for tourism and recreational activities. The picturesque surroundings, coupled with the expansive water body, attract visitors for boating, fishing, and sightseeing. The dam also contributes to local ecology by creating a habitat for various species of birds and aquatic life. Its strategic importance in regional development and energy production makes it a vital asset for both Punjab and the neighboring states, showcasing the intersection of engineering, environment, and community benefits.
